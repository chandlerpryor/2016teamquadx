The Trash Dash Trollbot

In order to train up the rookies, we created a game for them to compete in before the season started. It's based off the FRC game from last year, only scaled down for teaching the new hardware members. See here to see a more detailed description of our custom game.

Basket Pinchers

In order to grab onto and lift the basket, we mounted servo-powered "pinchers" that compress the sides of the box and can be released in order to stack more underneath.

Belt Drive

In order to power 2 wheels with a single motor, we decided to use a belt drive over a chain or gear system. This allows for fluid movement and less maintenance of the drive system.

Rack-and-Pinion Lift

We chose this lift system to increase the whole teams' exposure to different systems other than using pullyes, as well as deteremine whether it would be be a viable option for during the season.

After scrimmage, we realized that there was no need to climb over the churros if all you intend to do is hang. Instead, we made our robot very light and maneuverable yet rigid through CNCâ€™d wood panels. We have direct-to-drive powered wheels that allow us to be more fast and consistent on the ground than any other robot.

In order to get up the ramp without a bulky drive system, we needed a ltch that would hook us onto the first churro. From there, we can extend our lift to dump, or the claws will further rotate and push the front of the robot up to help us glide over the churros while hanging, completely negating the need to climb over the churros.

To keep us very light, we use aluminum slides and a separate winch mechanism that will hook onto the bar and lift us up, negating the need to make our actual lift overly long. Additionally, our basket is able to hold 5 pieces of debris and has low-friction rails that help extend our reach to the goal.

In order to manipulate debris, we use foam wrapped around tank treads. They allow us to pick up both blocks and balls very quickly and from hard to reach areas. Since the foam manipulator elevatest the debris to the basket above, it also creates a space of wiring and the control system.

Engineering Notebook
