Other Links

We've found these websites to be really helpful throughout the years.
  • CAD/Design
    • AutoDesk Inventor is great for CAD, and can be exported into Showcase for presentations.
      • Free Student Version of Inventor
      • Inventor Trenches: A blog with great tutorials.
    • PTC Creo is another application for CAD'ing, with a whole award on using it effectively.
      • PTC and FIRST PTC is even offereing grants to FTC teams this season!
      • Vertanux Training Manuals Huge PDFs on the basics of Creo, as well as other modelling software.
    • Blender is great for creating great renders and animations of your robot (like the ones in our promo), and is completely free.
      • Blender Tutorials: Their website has tons of resources on how to get started.
      • Introduction: This video walks through setting up the software.
  • Hardware
    • Suppliers are great for when you need more parts that what's provdided at the beginning of the season.
      • Servo City: Supplies Actobotics parts, a great alternative to Tetrix parts, as well as other supplies.
      • HiTechnic: All of the NXT sensors, which can still be used via the Legacy Module.
      • AndyMark: Tons of FTC-approved parts, as well as field kits.
    • 3D Printers allow for even greater customability.
      • Ekocycle Program: Last year, FIRST partnered with Cubify to provide Ekocycle printers to FTC teams that applied for the grant. Hopefully, they'll be doing the same thing this year!
      • MakerBot: If one's really invested in 3D printing as has a chunk of change they're willing to spend, the MakerBot is an affordable personal 3D printer.
  • Programming
    • Android Studios is the IDE that will be used to program the robots, starting this season.
      • Download: Setting up the IDE.
      • Intellitek Education: The only website that will walk you through programming with the Android Platform
      • FTC Forum: Since nobody knows much about the new platform, the forums will have all much of the info that's been released.
    • MIT App Inventor is an alterantive to Android Studios useful for teams that are just beginning to program in Java.
  • Engineering Notebook/Project Management
    • Physical Engineering Notebooks are what we prefer, because it allows for easy sketching and a certain level of tactile feedback to our progress.
      • Bound Notebook: The Amazon listing of our the EN we always get (plus, who doesn't love Prime?).
    • Digital or Online EN's have their own advantages, including neatness and easy access, and are increasingly popular. However, keep in mind an actual engineer's Notebook is a (rather messy), handwritten log of their entire thought process, including notes, brainstorming, and sketches.
      • Techno Warriors Advanced #3486: Their fantastic online EN is super detailed, written in by every team member, and clearly demonstrates the team's progress throughout the season.
      • FTC Engineering Logs: A great online tool developed specifically for FTC teams interested in maintaining an online Engineering Notebook.
      • Bounty Hunters #2864: Another great example of an EN from a couple of years ago.
    • Trello is a visual way of creating task lists and setting due dates for the team as a whole, as well as different subdivisions.
  • Competition Day
    • Scouting is vital in order to identify partners and opponents in upcoming rounds, as well as potential alliances during finals.
      • FTC Mobile Scouter: Developed by one of our team members, store PIT and stand scouting data on your phone and transfer to your computer for a spreadsheet with detailed info on every team's perfomance.
      • Scouting Form and Scouting Spreadsheet: courtesy of 4997 Masquerade, these are some of the most reliable in determining team performance without relying on the provided ranking system.
      • The Yellow Alliance: While the developers are currently redesigning some stuff, it's the only website that will provide extensive data on each team that makes it to Super Regionals and beyond.
  • Other Cool Stuff
    • FRC Rhapsody is exactly what it sounds like- a whole parody on life as a FIRST nerd. Trust us, it's great.
    • r/FTC: If Reddit is your thing, check out the whole subreddit of FTC goodness (and our resident expert, jspspike).
