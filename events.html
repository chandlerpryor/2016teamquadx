Community Outreach

Along with building a competitive robot, weâ€™ve made it one of our primary goals to promote STEM in our community.

  • FLL and FTC Mentoring

    On a weekly basis, we mentored the FLL team 6751 Team Gold, who made it to their Worlds Qualifier! We're super proud of these awesome guys.

  • Robot Demonstrations

    We've set up our field and driven around our Robot at countless events, including our school's faire "Viperpalooza," the yearly Austin Mini Maker Faire, and various elementary-school STEM fairs. We take advantage of these events in order to show off our hard work, but to also show the community how awesome robotics can be.

  • Youth STEM Programs

    Last season worked with Side By Side Kids, an after-school program for children in East Austin. We volunteered with SBSK every Thursday, tutoring student from Pre-K to 5th grade in Math and Science.

  • Workshops

    We've hosted workshops with the goal of introducing new resources to other FTC Teams. The software team personally organized an Introduction to GitHub workshop, and we've both hosted and attended ones on Leadership, Arduino board use, as well as even invited a guest speaker to come talk to us about some of the technology behind the Amazon Drone Project.
